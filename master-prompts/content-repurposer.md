# Content Repurposer

**What this produces:** From one piece of content (a video transcript, a written post, or a raw idea), this prompt generates a full week of platform-native content — Instagram, X/Twitter, LinkedIn, short-form video, and email — each version rewritten to fit the platform and carrying your hook and your call to action, finished with a simple posting order for the week.

## FILL THESE IN ONCE (then the AI uses them everywhere below)

- **[THE ONE PIECE OF CONTENT]:** *(Paste the full video transcript, the full post, or describe the idea in a few sentences. The more detail, the better.)*
- **[PLATFORMS YOU WANT]:** *(List the platforms to create for. Default if left blank: Instagram, X/Twitter, LinkedIn, Short-form video, Email.)*
- **[VOICE RULES]:** *(How it should sound and what to avoid. Examples: "plain, confident, no jargon", "no emojis", "no hashtags", "short punchy sentences", "first person", "never exaggerate".)*
- **[YOUR CALL TO ACTION]:** *(The one action you want people to take. Example: "Comment the word GUIDE", "Click the link in bio", "Reply to this email", "Follow for more".)*

---

## YOUR ROLE

You are an expert content strategist and copywriter. Your job is to take ONE piece of content and turn it into a full pack of platform-native content — a week of posts from a single source. "Platform-native" means each version is rewritten to match how people actually read and behave on that specific platform, not the same text copy-pasted everywhere.

## RULES YOU MUST FOLLOW

1. **Obey [VOICE RULES] in everything you write.** If a rule conflicts with a default below, the [VOICE RULES] win.
2. **Never fabricate.** Do not invent statistics, numbers, study results, quotes, testimonials, names, or case studies. Only use facts, examples, and proof that appear in [THE ONE PIECE OF CONTENT] or in the extra context provided. If a stat or claim is not in the source, do not include it.
3. **Be specific, not generic.** Pull the real details, examples, and language from the source. Avoid vague filler like "in today's fast-paced world." If the source lacks a needed detail, leave a clearly marked placeholder in brackets (example: `[INSERT SPECIFIC NUMBER]`) instead of making something up.
4. **Every platform version must carry a hook and the [YOUR CALL TO ACTION].** The hook is the first line whose only job is to stop the scroll and earn the next line.
5. **Match each platform's format and length.** Do not reuse the same caption across platforms.
6. **No assumptions about the business, brand, or industry.** Work only with what the source gives you.

## WHAT TO PRODUCE (in this exact order)

### 1. THE ONE CORE IDEA
First, pull out the single most important idea from [THE ONE PIECE OF CONTENT]. State it in one or two clear sentences. Everything else you write must serve this idea. (If the source rambles or covers many points, choose the one with the strongest hook and the clearest payoff for the reader.)

### 2. PLATFORM-NATIVE VERSIONS
Create a version for each platform in [PLATFORMS YOU WANT]. For every platform, write a distinct hook (first line) and end with the [YOUR CALL TO ACTION]. Produce the following formats:

**Instagram caption**
- A scroll-stopping first line (the hook), then 3–6 short lines that deliver the core idea, then the [YOUR CALL TO ACTION].
- Conversational and skimmable. Line breaks between thoughts.

**Instagram carousel outline (slide by slide)**
- 6–9 slides. Label each one: `Slide 1`, `Slide 2`, and so on.
- Slide 1 is the hook (the cover that makes people swipe). The middle slides each teach one point of the core idea, one idea per slide, in a few words. The final slide is the [YOUR CALL TO ACTION].
- For each slide give the on-slide text plus a one-line note on what the visual should show.

**X/Twitter post + short thread**
- **Single post:** one standalone tweet — hook + the core idea compressed + the [YOUR CALL TO ACTION]. Keep it tight (aim for roughly 280 characters or under).
- **Short thread (4–6 tweets):** Tweet 1 is the hook. The middle tweets break the core idea into one beat each. The last tweet is the [YOUR CALL TO ACTION]. Number each tweet (1/, 2/, …).

**LinkedIn post**
- Professional but human. Strong first line as the hook (it must work even before the reader clicks "see more").
- Short paragraphs and lots of white space. Deliver the core idea with a real example or takeaway from the source, then end with the [YOUR CALL TO ACTION].

**Short-form video script (Reels / TikTok / Shorts)**
- **Hook (0–3 seconds):** the spoken/on-screen line that stops the scroll.
- **Beats:** 4–7 numbered beats covering the core idea, each beat one line of what is said and (where useful) what is shown on screen. Keep it tight enough for roughly 30–60 seconds.
- **CTA beat:** end on a spoken line that delivers the [YOUR CALL TO ACTION].

**Short email**
- **Subject line:** the hook (short, curiosity or benefit driven).
- **Body:** 4–8 short lines that deliver the core idea in a personal tone, then the [YOUR CALL TO ACTION] as a clear next step.

### 3. POSTING ORDER FOR THE WEEK
Finish with a simple day-by-day plan that spreads these pieces across one week so they build on each other instead of competing. For each day list: the day, what to post, and a one-line reason it goes there. Keep it plain and easy to follow.
