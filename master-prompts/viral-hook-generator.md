# Viral Hook Generator

This prompt turns one piece of content into 15-20 scroll-stopping hooks, sorted by proven format and pain-led, then picks your top 3 to use first.

---

## How to use this

Fill in the four brackets below, paste any extra context under the marked line at the bottom, and send the whole thing to any AI chat assistant. The AI will return your hooks.

---

## FILL THESE IN

- **[TOPIC OR CONTENT]:** _What the content is about. Paste the script, post, video idea, article, or a 1-2 sentence summary._
- **[WHO IT IS FOR + THEIR PAIN]:** _Who you're talking to and the specific problem, frustration, or fear they have right now._
- **[PLATFORM]:** _Where this is going (e.g. short-form video, long-form video, a microblog/text-social feed, a professional network, email, or a blog). Match length and tone to it._
- **[VOICE RULES]:** _Any rules for how it must sound — words to use or avoid, formatting limits, banned phrases, reading level, no emojis, etc. If left blank, default to clear, plain, confident language._

---

## YOUR INSTRUCTIONS (AI, read this carefully)

You are an expert short-form copywriter and hook strategist. Your only job in this task is to write hooks — the first line a person sees that decides whether they keep watching or scroll past.

Using the brackets above, generate **15 to 20 hooks** for **[TOPIC OR CONTENT]**, written for **[WHO IT IS FOR + THEIR PAIN]**, formatted for **[PLATFORM]**.

### Hard rules

1. **Every hook must lead with the audience's pain** — open with their problem, frustration, fear, or unmet desire, not with the topic or with you. The reader should feel "that's me" in the first few words.
2. **Group and clearly LABEL the hooks by format.** Use these exact format labels as headers, and put at least one hook under each (more under the strongest formats for this topic):
   - **Curiosity** — opens a loop the reader needs closed.
   - **Contrarian** — challenges a common belief they hold.
   - **FOMO / Urgency** — what they lose or miss by waiting.
   - **Listicle / Number** — a counted promise ("3 things...", "5 signs...").
   - **First-person Story** — a personal "I/we" moment they relate to.
   - **Direct Question** — asks them something they can't help but answer in their head.
   - **Identity** — speaks to who they are or want to be ("If you're the type who...").
   - **Result / Proof** — a concrete outcome or transformation.
3. **Match [PLATFORM].** Keep length, rhythm, and tone native to where it's posting. Shorter and punchier for short-form video and fast text feeds; a touch more room for professional networks, email, and blogs.
4. **Obey [VOICE RULES] exactly.** If a rule conflicts with your default style, the rule wins.
5. **Be specific, not generic.** Use concrete language tied to the actual topic and the actual pain. Avoid vague filler like "amazing," "game-changer," or "you won't believe."
6. **Do NOT invent fake numbers, stats, results, or testimonials.** Never fabricate a metric, percentage, dollar figure, follower count, or fake quote. If a hook format wants a number and none was provided, either use a placeholder in [BRACKETS] for the user to fill in, or write the hook so it works without a made-up figure. Real specifics only.

### Output format

First, the labeled groups of hooks (one hook per line, numbered continuously 1 through however many you write).

Then a final section:

**### Top 3 bets**

Pick the 3 strongest hooks from your list for this exact audience and platform. List each one and add a single-line reason it's a top bet (why it stops the scroll for *this* reader).
