# SEO + GEO + AEO Generator

Turn any piece of content into a package that ranks in search **and** gets quoted by AI engines — built fresh for each client.

## The three things this optimizes for

| Term | What it means in one line |
|------|---------------------------|
| **SEO** (Search Engine Optimization) | Getting a page to rank in Google's blue links so people click it. |
| **GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization) | Getting your text *fed into* AI answers (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) as a source the model pulls from. |
| **AEO** (Answer Engine Optimization) | Getting your exact words *quoted back* as the answer in AI search and Google AI Overviews. |

GEO and AEO overlap heavily — both are about being the source AI trusts. SEO is the older game of ranking a clickable link. This skill does all three at once.

## How it works: per client, every time

This skill is brand-neutral. It produces nothing useful until it knows **whose** content it's optimizing. So step one is always the client's brand profile.

**Client Brand Profile (capture or ask for these):**

| Field | What to capture |
|-------|-----------------|
| Brand | The client's name / business name |
| Offer | What they actually sell (product, service, price point) |
| ICP + pain | Who buys, and the painful problem they're trying to solve |
| Money keyword | The one search/buyer phrase that signals purchase intent |
| Competitors | 2–4 alternatives the buyer compares against |
| Voice rules | Tone, words to use, and **banned words** (terms the client never uses) |
| Platforms | Where this gets published (e.g. Instagram, X, LinkedIn, YouTube, a blog) |

> **If the client profile is unknown, ask ONE tight question, then proceed.**
> Example: *"Who's this for, what do they sell, and who's their buyer (one line)?"*
> Don't interrogate. Make reasonable assumptions for anything missing and flag them in the output.

Once you have the profile, run the 9-step pipeline below. Everything in the output must reflect that client's brand, voice, and banned words.

## The 9-step pipeline

0. **READ** — Understand the input, the core idea, audience, offer, and funnel stage.
1. **SEO** — Keywords, searchable title, first line, on-screen text, alt-text.
2. **AEO** — The exact buyer questions to win.
3. **ANSWER BLOCK** — The quotable answer in the first 50 words.
4. **CITATION-BAIT** — 8–10 sentences engineered to be quoted verbatim.
5. **HOOKS** — Scroll-stopping openers (if it's social).
6. **TEXT-HOME VERSION** — The crawlable page AI actually reads and cites.
7. **PLATFORM PACK** — Ready-to-post copy per platform.
8. **SCORE & NEXT** — AI-citation readiness, gaps, and where to publish.

## Output structure

Return clean Markdown under these exact headers, in order:
`## 0. READ` · `## 1. SEO` · `## 2. AEO` · `## 3. ANSWER BLOCK` · `## 4. CITATION-BAIT` · `## 5. HOOKS` · `## 6. TEXT-HOME VERSION` · `## 7. PLATFORM PACK` · `## 8. SCORE & NEXT`

> **The GEO insight that changes everything:** AI engines cite **crawlable TEXT** — a webpage, a blog post, a LinkedIn article, a YouTube description, a Reddit/Quora answer. They do **not** reliably read social captions, image text, or video pixels. That means the **Text-Home Version (Step 6) is the single highest-value output**, and it only earns citations if it's actually **published somewhere crawlable**. A perfect caption with no text home gets likes; a published text home gets cited. Always tell the client where to put it.

## After generating — self-check (run before you hand it over)

- **Answer test:** Does the ANSWER BLOCK directly answer the ONE primary buyer question in the first 50 words, with no throat-clearing?
- **Self-contained test:** Can each CITATION-BAIT line stand alone with zero surrounding context and still make sense and name the brand/offer?
- **Banned-words test:** Did any of the client's banned words (or off-voice phrasing) slip into ANY section? Strip them.
- **No-fabrication test:** Are all stats and claims real or clearly framed as the client's own claim — nothing invented?

If any check fails, fix it before delivering.
